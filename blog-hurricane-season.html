How to Prepare Your Roof for Texas Hurricane Season

üìÖ January 10, 2026 ‚è±Ô∏è 6 min read By RoofEdge Team

Hurricane season in Texas runs from June 1st through November 30th, bringing the potential for severe wind damage, heavy rainfall, and flying debris. Proper roof preparation can mean the difference between minor repairs and catastrophic damage.

Start with a Professional Inspection

Before hurricane season begins, schedule a comprehensive roof inspection with a licensed contractor. A professional can identify vulnerabilities that homeowners often miss:

  • Loose or damaged shingles that could blow off in high winds
  • Compromised flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights
  • Weak spots in the roof deck that need reinforcement
  • Deteriorated sealants that could allow water intrusion

Reinforce Vulnerable Areas

Once you've identified weak points, prioritize these critical reinforcements:

1. Secure Loose Shingles

Even a few loose shingles can create a domino effect during high winds. Wind-driven rain can infiltrate under lifted edges, causing water damage and further weakening the roofing structure.

2. Check and Seal Flashing

Flashing is your roof's first line of defense against water intrusion. Make sure all flashing is properly secured and sealed with high-quality roofing cement.

3. Trim Overhanging Branches

Trees near your home should be trimmed back at least 10 feet from your roofline. During hurricane-force winds, even healthy branches can become dangerous projectiles.

4. Clean Your Gutters

Clogged gutters prevent proper drainage during heavy rainfall. This can cause water to back up under your shingles and into your home. Clean gutters thoroughly and ensure downspouts direct water away from your foundation.

Emergency Preparedness Checklist

Keep these supplies on hand before hurricane season:

  • Heavy-duty tarps (at least 6-mil thickness)
  • Plywood sheets for emergency repairs
  • Roofing cement and caulk
  • Hammer, nails, and basic tools
  • Safety equipment (gloves, goggles, hard hat)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Safety Note

Never attempt roof repairs during a storm or immediately after when winds are still high. Wait until conditions are completely safe, and always contact professionals for post-storm damage assessment.

Post-Storm Inspection Protocol

After a hurricane passes, conduct a thorough inspection:

  1. Visual inspection from the ground: Look for obvious damage like missing shingles, dents, or debris
  2. Check your attic: Look for water stains, daylight coming through, or wet insulation
  3. Document everything: Take photos and videos for insurance claims
  4. Call professionals immediately: Don't wait‚Äîstorm damage worsens quickly

Insurance Considerations

Understanding your insurance coverage before hurricane season is critical:

  • Review your homeowner's policy deductible and coverage limits
  • Confirm whether wind damage and water intrusion are covered
  • Keep detailed records of all maintenance and repairs
  • Take photos of your roof's condition before the season starts
